HIGH LIKELIHOOD IDENTIFICATION OF PHOSPHOPEPTIDES

This search form accepts a list of tryptic peptide masses (entered as monoisotopic, MH+ masses) and the mass measurement error, and returns a list of candidate peptides having that mass, ordered by their probability of phosphorylation.

The user can drill down deeper into the output to determine the sequences of the candidate peptides whose mass falls within the mass measurement error, along with annotation data regarding the source.

This database contains tryptic peptides derived from the human and mouse subsets of the IPI protein database (v.3.07). A peptide can have up to two missed cleavage site. Cysteines are assumed to be carboxymethylated. Peptides are allowed to be variably phosphorylated at 1,2, or 3 potential phosphorylation sites on S, T and Y residues and to be variably oxidized at methionine residues.
